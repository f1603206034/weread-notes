# 61. Liberty

> 📌 In proclaiming the power of the common people, many of the followers of Mao Tze-tung put forth doctrines more characteristic of the MiddleAges than of Marxism. “Follow the masses," they say, proclaiming thatthe people are always good, that the people can do no wrong. Thus theypave the way to absolutism, for obviously if the people can do no wrong,then the power that represents the people can also do no wrong.
Mao Tze-tung has fought against these theories, but sincethe Com-munists continue to refer to the “people,”it is apparent that the in-dividual part of the populace must subordinate itself to the wholepopulace. By setting up the people as a transcendent principle, above alland beyond all, the Communists have created an atmosphere in whichtheir power can expand without inhibition. Everything must be rendered to the people, that is to the sovereign, to power itself.
